How It Works

The Refugee Learning Accelerator is a six month program, lasting from October 2017 to Spring 2018. It has three phases. Teams will be selected to advance through the phases on the basis of their performance.

Ideate

Online, October 2017-November 2017

Time commitment: 15-20 hours/week

Teams will work through a series of design challenges, aimed at helping them to strengthen their technical skills, expand their familiarity with digital pedagogy and deepen their understanding of the needs of refugee learners. Teams will have access to digital resources, online courses, calls with mentors, and extensive feedback.

At the end of the Ideate phase, successful teams will be selected to move forward to the Prototype phase.

Prototype

In person, January 2018

Time commitment: full-time week-long workshop

Successful teams will be brought together in Amman for an in-person workshop. Teams will be guided through the design thinking process and--building on the skills and knowledge they gained in the Ideate stage--come up with an idea to prototype. Teams will have access to experts in the field, ...

At the end of the Prototype phase, teams will pitch for follow-on support during the Incubate stage.

Incubate

Blended, February 2018-April 2018

Time commitment: part-time

Up to ten teams will be selected for the Incubate stage. They will receive funding (up to $10,000), mentorship and individualized support to further develop their prototype. This could include: technical mentorship from expert developers; help establishing partnerships with local universities, NGOs or international organizations; support designing an initial evaluation to show project impact, etc.,
